# HR Analytics Dashboard

## Overview

This project analyzes employee attrition patterns using SQL and Power BI.

The objective is to identify factors contributing to employee attrition and provide actionable insights for HR decision-making.

---

## Tools Used

- MySQL
- Power BI
- Excel

---

## Key Metrics

- Total Employees
- Attrition Count
- Attrition Rate
- Average Age
- Average Monthly Income

---

## Analysis Performed

### Executive Summary

- Employee Attrition by Department
- Employee Attrition by Job Role
- Employee Attrition by Gender
- Employee Attrition by Age Group
- Employee Attrition by Education Field

### Employee Insights

- Employee Attrition by Overtime
- Employee Attrition by Work-Life Balance
- Employee Attrition by Job Satisfaction
- Average Monthly Income by Department

---

## Key Findings

- Research & Development department recorded the highest attrition.
- Laboratory Technicians showed the highest attrition among job roles.
- Employees aged 30-40 contributed the largest share of attrition.
- Male employees had higher attrition compared to female employees.
- Life Sciences employees showed the highest attrition among education fields.
- Employees working overtime experienced significantly higher attrition.
- Work-life balance and job satisfaction had a noticeable impact on employee retention.
